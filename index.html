Stop Before You Break It: Leveraging One Class Anomaly Detection for Safer Imitation Learning Policies

Anonymous
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

We introduce Safe-pi, a framework aimed at improving the reliability and safety of Imitation Learning-based policies. After having aggregated expert demonstrations representations, it continuously extracts anomaly scores from real time policy-robot interactions. When a significant deviation is detected, the system flags an anomaly and generates a heatmap to localize anomaly.

Abstract

Recent advances in Imitation Learning have significantly expanded the capabilities of autonomous robots. However, their deployment is limited by the non-deterministic behavior of generative policies and the overall lack of error-checking mechanisms on policies and their outcomes, especially in the presence of unpredictable and out-of-distribution events. We propose Safe-pi, a post-hoc One-Class Anomaly Detection framework designed to enhance the safety of Imitation Learning policies, and help identify their shortcomings. Taking inspiration from One-Class Visual Anomaly Detection, our method builds a memory of nominal behaviors from expert demonstrations using Gaussian statistics over spatially localized visual features, without requiring anomalous samples. During inference, the system monitors robot perceptions in real time, identifying anomalies in both the robot's behavior and its environment. We also introduce BotFails, a multimodal dataset of real-world tasks for Anomaly Detection in robotics, and demonstrate that Safe-pi outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, offering robust and interpretable failure detection suitable for practical deployment.
